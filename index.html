CHAPTER 1

Physical
Distance

You wanna sit and chat with one another. And that fundamentally changes when that sitting together and talking to each other is over Facetime or over a phone call.

TIPS: Drag the dots to each side.

You can’t be physically intimate

because you are so far away.

LDRR starts from the physical distance. Two persons are being separated from each other. With this distance, you are not able to meet each other as frequently as usual. With this distance, you cannot be there instantly when the other person needs you, even though what they need is just a simple hug…

Time

So I need to use my lunch break quickly, finish my meal, sneak into the meeting room, call her for like 10 minutes, and go back to work right after that…

It is all about negotiation

and compromise.

In a LDRR, time difference could be one of the most annoying problems for some couples. You’ll always need to coordinate with the other person’s schedule, calculating the time and waiting for them.

Technology can be irritating.

In a LDRR, you rely so much on various communication technologies to connect with your partner. However, when you both finally get some time talking to each other, technical problems could always make your lives even harder...

CHAPTER 4

Negative
Thoughts

How come he hasn’t told me if he’s home? Is he in danger? Or maybe he is just having fun with his friends and totally forgetting about me…

TIPS: Whack negative thoughts! Press the HEART button to start.

TRUST is the key.

Once you fail to contact the other person, you might start thinking of some negative situations. It is totally normal. But try not to freak yourself out. After all, apart from believing in the other person, there is nothing you can do really.

When you finally reach them, maybe talk to them and see if there is anything they can help to improve this situation. For some couples, they use “share locations”, but for other couples, this may be unnecessary. There is no right or wrong way. Only if you both accept it, it is a good way.

CHAPTER 5

Loneliness

I feel like I am the only one who is alone by myself…

TIPS: Move your mouse. Drive away other dots.

Deal with loneliness.

Of course there will be some hard times that you feel incredibly lonely, especially when you look around and see that couples are everywhere, having sweet moments together.

Well, why not take this as an opportunity to become independent? Try to enjoy your own time and space, exploring things you like, turning yourself into a better person.

CHAPTER 6

Memorable
Object

When I wear this ring, it makes me think of him. I feel like he is always here with me. It’s a kind of feeling that is hard to explain. I feel secure and loved.

TIPS: Press and hold the dot in the middle utill the hreatbeats from both sides meet each other.

Keep the memories.

Being apart from the other person, some people keep a specific thing that reminds them of their partners. Sometimes it is not about the object itself but the stories and memories behind it. These could actually be the resources for maintaining a sense of a relationship and a positive attitude toward their partners.

CHAPTER 7

Life
Sharing

Communicate to maintain

the intamacy

Since there is already a lack of physical contact in a LDRR, communication becomes even more important than before. The more things you share, the more you trust each other, the more secure and intimate you feel.

Besides daily conversation, experts suggest that couples talk about serious topics rather than avoiding potential areas of disagreement during their interaction.

CHAPTER 8

Anticipation

You definitely, in distance, develop two separate lives that you hope can come together at some point.

TIPS: Connects all dots on the board. Connection are made vertically or horizontally, and without overlap.

A foreseen future is crucial.

In a LDRR, uncertainty is always one of the biggest challenges between couples. A foreseen future could be a huge motivation for you to keep the relationship going. Too early to say? Don’t worry. It is OK that you can’t foresee your future for now. But what you could do is to build up the anticipation of your reunion.

No matter how advanced the technology is, it is unlikely that mediated means could totally replace face to face interactions. Research shows that the more face to face interaction couples have, the more satisfied they are in their LDRR. So why not plan the next meeting time together. Be prepared and be excited!
